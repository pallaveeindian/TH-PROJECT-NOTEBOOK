History Background of Uttar Pradesh State Rural Livelihood Mission (UPSRLM)
The Uttar Pradesh State Rural Livelihood Mission (UPSRLM) was established to address rural poverty and unemployment by creating sustainable livelihood opportunities through social mobilization, financial inclusion, and capacity building. Its journey is a story of empowerment, resilience, and community-driven development across rural Uttar Pradesh. Below is a detailed account of the key milestones in its history:

Inception and Foundation (2012)
The UPSRLM was launched in 2012 as a part of the National Rural Livelihood Mission (NRLM), which was initiated by the Government of India under the Ministry of Rural Development.
The mission’s primary objective was to alleviate rural poverty by organizing poor households into Self-Help Groups (SHGs) and providing them with access to financial resources, training, and sustainable livelihood opportunities.
Initial Phase (2012 - 2014)
Focused on creating awareness and social mobilization in rural areas.
Conducted baseline surveys to identify poor households and their specific needs.
Began forming SHGs with an emphasis on women's empowerment.
Partnered with local banks to initiate financial inclusion efforts.
Trained community resource persons to help mobilize and guide SHGs.
Expansion and Capacity Building (2015 - 2018)
Significant growth in the number of SHGs across all districts of Uttar Pradesh.
Expanded focus to include multiple livelihood opportunities, such as agriculture, dairy farming, and handicrafts.
Established Village Organizations (VOs) and Cluster Level Federations (CLFs) to support SHG activities.
Launched various capacity-building programs to improve the financial literacy and entrepreneurial skills of SHG members.
Developed a robust partnership with Rural Self-Employment Training Institutes (RSETIs) for skill development and job creation.
Digital Transformation (2019 - 2021)
Introduced digital platforms to facilitate SHG transactions and improve transparency.
Launched a mobile app for SHG members to track savings, loans, and livelihood activities.
Integrated the MIS (Management Information System) for real-time monitoring and reporting of program outcomes.
Promoted digital literacy programs for SHG members to enable e-banking and online marketing of SHG products.
Established digital marketing channels for promoting SHG products on e-commerce platforms.
COVID-19 Response and Resilience Building (2020)
During the COVID-19 pandemic, UPSRLM played a vital role in distributing essential supplies and creating income opportunities through the production of masks and sanitizers by SHG members.
Introduced online training programs to ensure continuity of skill development activities.
Focused on building resilience among rural communities to cope with economic challenges.
Strengthening Market Linkages (2022 - Present)
Emphasis shifted towards creating strong market linkages to enhance the income of SHG members.
Organized regular fairs, exhibitions, and trade events to promote SHG products.
Collaborated with online marketplaces and government e-commerce platforms for product promotion.
Launched several brand-building initiatives for SHG products under the “One District One Product” (ODOP) scheme.
Future Vision (2025 and Beyond)
Aims to make SHGs self-sustaining and independent by 2026.
Plans to introduce more innovative livelihood activities, such as sustainable agriculture, renewable energy-based businesses, and digital entrepreneurship.
Will continue promoting social inclusion by focusing on vulnerable groups, including women, tribal communities, and persons with disabilities.
Plans to adopt cutting-edge technologies, such as AI and big data, for more effective monitoring and decision-making.
Impact and Achievements
Formation of over 5 million SHGs across all districts of Uttar Pradesh.
Enhanced the livelihoods of millions of rural households through increased access to credit, markets, and skill development.
Empowered women to take leadership roles and make financial decisions independently.
Improved financial inclusion with more than 95% SHG members accessing formal banking services.
